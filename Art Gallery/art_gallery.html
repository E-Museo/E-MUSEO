PAINTING

Featured Works

The Parisian Life
Juan Luna

"A Parisian Life" (or "Une vie parisienne") is a captivating painting that offers a glimpse into the vibrant and bustling atmosphere of late 19th-century Paris. Created by Jean Béraud, the artwork showcases a snapshot of daily life in the city, capturing the essence of Parisian society during that era.

View More
The First Mass
Carlos Modesto Francisco

As seen, the painting portrays the first mass which was celebrated in Limasawa Island on the day of Easter. It is believed in the painting that whatever is the person's nationality, as long as that person was there to attend the mass, that person is welcomed and fully accepted.

The Departure of the Fisherman

We see here then the fishermen as embodiments of determination - indomitable spirits charging forward in spite of the storms of circumstance.

The Madonna of the Slums
Vicente Manansala

Highlights the soft, feminine curve of the mother's neck and shoulders. The mother is portrayed as gazing down towards her child, displaying the protective instinct a mother has for her baby.

Planting Rice

Spolarium
Juan Luna

The painting portrays a gripping and dramatic scene within the depths of a Roman coliseum. The central focus is a massive spoliarium, a chamber where the bodies of fallen gladiators were brought for stripping of their armor and disposal.
