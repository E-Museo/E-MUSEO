ARCHITECTURE

San Agustin Church

INTRAMUROS MANILA

GOTHIC STYLE

San Agustin Church is a remarkable example of Filipino Gothic architecture located within Manila's old walled city of Intramuros. The church was built during the Spanish colonial period and is distinguished by its majestic exterior, embellished with elaborate stone carvings and a rose window. Soaring arches, ribbed vaults, and thin columns create a sense of verticality and grandeur in the interior. The superb Baroque-style retablo and ornate altars of the church add to its beauty and holy significance.

Manila Cathedral

INTRAMUROS MANILA

GOTHIC STYLE

Another outstanding example of Filipino Gothic architecture is the Manila Cathedral, officially known as the Cathedral-Basilica of the Immaculate Conception. The cathedral, located in Intramuros, Manila, is a grand structure with an imposing front and twin bell towers. The façade is distinguished by pointed arches, ornate spires, and exquisite tracery, while the interior is distinguished by a tall nave, ribbed vaults, and superb stained glass windows.

Paoay Church

PAOAY, ILOCOS NORTE

BAROQUE STYLE

Paoay Church, also known as the Saint Augustine Church of Paoay, is an iconic example of Filipino Baroque architecture. It is made of coral stones and features massive buttresses on its sides. The church's façade showcases intricate details and ornamentation, with arches, volutes, and niches.

Vigan

VIGAN CITY, ILOCOS SUR

EUROPEAN COLONIAL ARCHITECTURE

Vigan is a city located in the province of Ilocos Sur in the northern Philippines. It is known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture and cultural heritage. The city was founded in the 16th century by the Spanish conquistador Juan de Salcedo, and served as a center of trade and commerce during the spanish colonial period. Many of the buildings in Vigan date back to the 18th and 19th centuries, and reflect the unique blend of Spanish and Asian influence that characterizes the Philippines colonial Architecture.
