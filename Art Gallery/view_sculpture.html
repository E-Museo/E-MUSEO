SCULPTURE

The Oblation

Guillermo Tolentino

Synthesis of classical and modern inspirations

The Oblation is a bronze sculpture located in the University of the Philippines Diliman Campus in Quezon City, Philippines. It was created by the Italian sculptor, Francesco Ricardo Monti, and was unveiled in 1935. The sculpture depicts a man, who represents the Filipino youth, holding up an offering or sacrifice towards the heavens with his arms outstretched. The figure is depicted as strong and muscular, with his head thrown back and his eyes closed in an expression of reverence and dedication

SCULPTURE

Jose Rizal Monument

Richard Kissling

BAROQUE STYLE

The Jose Rizal Monument is a famous landmark located in Manila, the capital city of the Philippines. It is dedicated to Dr. Jose Rizal, a national hero and prominent figure in the country's history. It was designed by the Swiss sculptor Richard Kissling and was unveiled on December 30, 1913. The monument stands in Rizal Park formerly known as Luneta Park, which is a historical urban park that spans an area of 58 hectares. The park is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike and serves as a venue for various events and gatherings. The monument consists of a bronze statue of Jose Rizal standing atop a granite pedestal. Rizal is depicted holding a book in his left hand and a quill pen in his right hand, symbolizing his contributions as a writer and intellectual.

SCULPTURE

Bonifacio National Monument

Guillermo Tolentino

Classical

Bonifacio National Movement is a large bronze and concrete sculpture located in Caloocan City, Philippines. It was designed by the Filipino sculptor, Guillermo Tolentino, and unveiled in 1933. The monument commemorates the life and legacy of Andres Bonifacio, a key figure in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule. The sculpture depicts Bonifacio holding a bolo in one hand and a flag in the other, symbolizing his leadership in the revolution and his commitment to the cause of Philippine Independence.

SCULPTURE

The Triupmph of Science Over Death

Guillermo Tolentino

Neoclassical

"The Triumph of Science Over Death" is a monumental sculpture that depicts a female figure holding a torch and a book, representing the triumph of knowledge and enlightenment over death and ignorance. The sculpture showcases intricate details and dynamic movement, with the figure standing on a pedestal adorned with symbolic elements.

SCULPTURE

Kasaysayan ng Lahi

Eduardo Castrillo

Contemporary/Symbolic

"Kasaysayan ng Lahi" is a massive sculpture depicting important events in Philippine history. The sculpture is made up of numerous interconnecting metal panels, each with a different scene and emblem reflecting a particular epoch or milestone in Filipino history. It's a visual tale that captures the essence of the Filipino people's struggles, victories, and cultural heritage.
